I am a graphic design student who is interested in growing and developing my skills as an artist and creative. This assignment was to demonstrate our understanding of the Swiss aesthetic in design. This poster was constructed using grids, a visual hierarchy, lineals, and a limited palette of 2 colors. This poster was printed on white paper advertising a scientific seminar which took place both online and in person.

This assignment was to design a poster centered around one of the 17 global sustainable development goals. My poster is centered around Life Below Water. I designed the fishhook to draw attention to the issues with overfishing, and the dangerous impacts this habit has on the environment. I drew a school of fish in the background, playing around with varying sizes and colors to create a sense of depth.

This was another personal project where I experimented with optical illusions. I developed a system that allowed me to construct this effect without getting overwhelmed during the process. I constructed a template which I used to design symbols which I then pasted many times over to create a grid. I then made lots of colored bubbles to take up space behind the grid in various patterns, which is what actually creates the illusion of movement where there is none.
